causes of climate change

The main contributor to climate change is human activity, which releases greenhouse gases into the atmosphere, trapping heat and causing global warming. The main causes include the usage of halocarbons, deforestation, agriculture (especially cattle farming), industrial processes, waste management, and the burning of fossil fuels for energy and transportation. These activities increase the greenhouse effect by releasing more greenhouse gases such as carbon dioxide, methane, nitrous oxide, and others.

marginalised vs privileged

marginalised women:

Women who are already marginalised are especially affected by climate change, which causes economic vulnerability and gender inequality. Due to their responsibilities for supporting their families with food, water, and fuel, women frequently face the majority of the issues brought on by the climate in many developing nations. With shifting weather patterns, it becomes more challenging to acquire natural resources, which increases the workload for women, requires working longer hours to obtain essentials, and leaves less time for education and income-generating activities. Furthermore, disasters brought on by climate change have the potential to destroy communities, making women more susceptible to abuse and exploitation. Women's capacity to adapt to climate change and take part in measures to promote resilience is further restricted by limited access to resources and decision-making authority.

privileged women:

Although privileged women are affected by climate change, they may have more resources and chances for adaptation than marginalised women have. Due to major weather disasters like hurricanes or wildfires, they could experience lifestyle interruptions and financial difficulties. However, their access to social networks, financial resources, and educational opportunities may help them adapt more successfully to the changing environment. Nevertheless, all women are impacted by climate change; therefore, tackling its consequences on both privileged and marginalised women alike requires raising awareness, cooperation, and gender-inclusive policy.
